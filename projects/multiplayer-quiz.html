Multiplayer Quiz

Tech: Javascript, React, socket.io, Nodemon

Visit Site Linkedin Post

I build this in order to host a quiz at work. The goal was to have two teams each sitting in groups around their one laptop (per team). I would then have my laptop (the quizmaster) on a big screen for everyone to see. So just to reiterate. There are two laptops and a third (mine), which is the controller.

This means I had to distinguish in the frontend logic, which ones were the players and which was the controller.

This was actually not that hard, as I have the state stored on the server. So one team adds their name and is now team 1, the next team adds their name and is team 2, that means any other browser with socket ids not matching either of those can be the controller. (I wasn't worried about other people joining as this was a one off use)

I utilised the library socket.io, which I was vaguely familiar with from my bootcamp project. web sockets in order to keep the game state in the server.

Made in React.
